GUIDELINES AND TEMPLATE FOR UNDERGRADUATE PROJECTS

A. POINTS TO NOTE
All chapter headings and preliminary page headings should be font size 14, while every other text should be 12 except otherwise stated. Font type is Times New Roman. Paper quality of the four hard copies to be submitted (with a CD each) must be good quality not less than 80g/m2. Size of paper should be quarto and photocopies should be permanent. Margin should be 1”/2.54cm (for top, bottom and right) and 1.5”/3.81cm (for left). Spacing is double line.

B. PRELIMINARIES AND CONTENT ARRANGEMENT (Should be numbered with Roman numerals)

TITLE PAGE
The first (unnumbered) page after the fly leaf shall bear the full title of the project, the full name of the student in surname, other name(s) order and the month and year of submission of the project. Font size should be 16 points, ALL CAPS, bold, centralized and double spaced.

CERTIFICATION PAGE
This page shall show a statement signed by the supervisor(s) to the effect that materials recorded in the project resulted from research carried out by the student, under supervision (see Appendix 3).

DEDICATION
This page shall show a statement indicating who the project is dedicated to.

ACKNOWLEDGEMENT
The acknowledgment of aid received during the preparation of the project and individuals directly or indirectly involved in the project with a brief statement of what their contribution was.  

TABLE OF CONTENTS
This shall contain the contents of the project starting from the title page, section and sub-section headings must be included, along with the page numbers on which these sections begin. It should be Single line spacing and page numbers must be aligned.

LIST OF TABLES
Starting on a fresh page, provide a complete list of all cited tables, showing table captions and page numbers in numerical order.

LIST OF FIGURES
Starting on a fresh page, provide a complete list of all cited figures, showing figure captions and page numbers in numerical order.

ABBREVIATIONS AND ACRONYMS
Where appropriate, a list of main abbreviations (with full meanings) should be provided. Standard abbreviations should be adhered to.

ABSTRACT
A short abstract of not more than 500 words, Single spaced, one block, one page, Should briefly touch on introduction, objectives, methodology, findings, conclusion and recommendation.

C. MAIN BODY OF THE PROJECT 
(Should be numbered with Arabic numerals e.g. 1.1, 2.1,  3.1….. ...)
Indent the first line of each paragraph by 0.5 inches. Use double line spacing throughout the main body of the document except where otherwise stated. Do not add extra spaces between paragraphs. Chapter headings should be size 14, upper case, bold and centralized. Section and sub-section headings should be initial caps (key words only) and bold. Cite references with the (author, date) format i.e APA referencing style.  For example, Monday (1996) states that …, or, the consequences of plagiarism can be personal, professional, ethical, and legal (Monday, 1996).”  Remember that “et al.” is an abbreviation for  ‘and others ” so there is a period after al., but not after et, before using et al., all authors should be cited in line with APA 7th edition in text-citation style . Do not use first person singular (“I”) or plural (“we”). All tables should be single line spacing. Use scientific writing style and avoid the use of slangs like gotten, 

CHAPTER ONE
INTRODUCTION
Background to the Study: Give background information on the topic at hand. Define your concepts, and show the interconnection amongst the variables in the topic. Emphasize the rationale underlying the study and its importance. A good introduction should be able to demonstrate that a research problem does exist.
 Statement of the Problem: A very concise and compact statement of the problem should be written not more than 3/4 of a page. A good statement of the problem should state that a genuine research problem exists which can be solved through research.
Objectives of the Study: Generate research objectives in line with the topic under investigation.
Research Questions (Where applicable):  Research questions should be raised in line with the objectives earlier stated. Please note that projects on design and implementation topics should not have research questions. 
Scope of the Study: Scope of the study should be concise and compact. This should comprise of the purpose of the study taking into consideration the target population, location of the study and other information distinct to the study. There is a need to justify the selection of the target population,  location among others.  
Limitation of the Study (Where applicable): These are restrictions imposed by the environment of the study area. E.g sampling frame, sampling technique, instrumentation among others.
Significance of the Study- This centers on the benefits of your study to relevant stakeholders. There is the need to justify the importance of each of the benefits.
Project Report Structure- Segmentalize your project work into five chapters, explaining briefly the focus of each of the chapters.
